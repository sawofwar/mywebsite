20/12

We've finally been getting some sunlight over the past few days! A welcome change from the constantly grey sky and the thick fog.

I've applied for my first programming internships, so fingers crossed.

Got to try out some basic local food, khachapuri and khinkali. Pity I can't drink the local wines.

We have to keep looking for coins to use our lift. It'd bee too much of a strain on my knees to walk to the 12th (11th) floor.

My sleeping schedule's completely off because of all the work and trying to learn and develop my projects.

The city's very crowded when we go outside. I've never lived in a place like this, in a capital. There's lots of people everywhere hurrying to their jobs, to the shopping centres, and, simply, everywhere.

10/12

In December, Tbilisi is very cold compared to cities on the south coast of Turkey.

We are renting a big old Soviet flat. The Airbnb host is lovely, but the musty smell's been getting on my nerves.

On our first day, I got food poisoning from cheap khinkali. Still haven't got over that. Also, yesterday our host was very kind to bring me pills from the chemist's.

Still, there's no poetry when you're exhausted by illness. No art.

A grey sky and a thick fog every day.

Stayed in all day. Marina had lots of work to do, and I took a day off from all the work I've been doing.

We are leaving soon, and I can't help feeling a little sad about it. I think I've got used to the calm stability of living in this nice bungalow with our amazing host and his family next door.

Over the past week, I've been trying to learn Turkish. It was only when our fourth week here started when I realised I could pick up some of the language. It turned out very useful too. Now I can even have a micro-conversation with our neighbours and their family!

Tonight, we opened the curtain behind our glass door and saw a stray dog frantically wagging its tail. When we got out, it gave us the saddest of looks and started whimpering. I had to feed it the rest of my delicious salami; if I hadn't, Marina would've never forgiven me, haha!

Now it's 4 AM, and the dog is sleeping on our balcony. Don't really know what to feel about having this new unexpected little friend.
